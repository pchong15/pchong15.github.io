Re-Connect

Re-Connect App Idea

  • July 2019 | App Design

About Re-Connect

Re-Connect is an app idea for consumers to find discounts based on location data. Users will find discounted deals around their area and be able to pick-up their items to avoid lines.

Problem Overview

YoY sales for department stores are down 5.9% since last year, while online retailers’ sales are up 11.9% over the last year. ( Source). People are looking for a convenient way to purchase products through technology and are avoiding brick and motors.

There is a need for store owners to increase sales through technology that allows consumers a convenient way to get their purchases.

Research

I interviewed a total of 10 people between the age of 19-55 to get their input on how they shop.

Biggest takeaways:

  • 7/10 interviewees felt that online shopping was more convenient that going out.
  • 10/10 interviewees still go to department stores
  • 8/10 interviewees felt that they found better deals online
The goal –to find a way to drive business to retailers and help consumers find better deals and shop more conveniently.

Approach

Create a marketplace for consumers to conveniently pick-up items that they need at a discounted rate.

“Curbside is becoming an increasingly common omnichannel delivery option, especially in the grocery vertical. A recent consumer survey from the National Retail Federation (NRF) suggests shoppers are receptive – 63% of respondents would like to use curbside pickup and 27% have tried it.” ( Source).

There are 2 simple value propositions for consumers:

1. Receive there purchased items quicker.
2. Get the items at a cheaper rate

Note: Ideally the app will be populated as a dual marketplace with businessowners populating their own discounts and confirming they have a pick-up service, however initial launch could scrape discount websites and cross check them with stores that have pick-up service to allow a larger data-set at launch.

Initial Design

The initial design is created with a few things in mind: location, discount %, and the user’s favorite categories

Note: favorite categories of the users can be found through an onboarding screen prior to getting on the app and then a score can be created per category based on how long a user interacts with categories.

Based on an aggregate score of the user’s want, the app will show the most wanted deal (based on category page) to the user at the very top, followed by a card design of trending sales of user's want.

When the user clicks on a deal, they will be able to see a product description, nearest store location, QR code for the discount. The QR code can be used when going to the store, but the user can opt into the option of a pick-up service (think of a curbside pick-up like target). When the user hits pick-up, they will be sent to a check-out page. The app will let the user know when it’s ready and the user will simply just alert the app letting the store know that they are on their way or that they are there.

Final Thoughts

Further research must be conducted to see if small/local businesses can support pick-up. Ideally there should be a separate portal for business owners to get notifications of customers coming to the store for pick-up.
Will need to consider what happens when the owner doesn’t have his or her phone to receive notification –this will lose seamless purchasing and add a lag time if owners aren’t aware of purchases for users.
Additional research of successful curb-side pick-up services like target and Walmart should be conducted
